# Contoso Corp New Hire Onboarding Guide

## Before Your First Day

Welcome to Contoso Corp. The period between accepting your offer and walking through the door on day one is designed to remove friction so that your first day can focus on people and purpose rather than paperwork. Your recruiter and your hiring manager will both stay in touch during this window, but the HR Portal is where most of the substantive work happens, and you should plan to log in within 48 hours of accepting your offer to begin the pre-boarding checklist.

The first item in the HR Portal checklist is identity and eligibility verification. You will be asked to complete Section 1 of Form I-9 electronically no later than three business days before your start date, and to schedule an in-person or notarized remote document review with a designated HR representative so that Section 2 can be completed on or before your actual start date. Employees who cannot complete verification within the federally required window will have their start date adjusted; this is a compliance requirement, not a Contoso Corp preference, and HR cannot waive it under any circumstances.

Alongside I-9 verification, you will complete a standard background check consent form through our third-party screening vendor. Most checks, including employment history, education verification, and criminal background screening where legally permitted, are completed within five to seven business days. Roles with financial systems access, physical facility security responsibilities, or client data handling duties may also require a supplemental credit or reference check, which your recruiter will flag for you specifically if it applies to your position.

You will also set up direct deposit and complete federal and state tax withholding elections (Form W-4 and the applicable state equivalent) through the HR Portal's payroll module. Contoso Corp pays on a semi-monthly cycle, on the 15th and last business day of each month, and your first paycheck will typically arrive on the first regular pay date that falls at least five business days after your start date. If direct deposit setup is not complete by the payroll processing deadline, your first payment will be issued as a physical check mailed to your address on file.

Equipment provisioning begins as soon as your background check consent and I-9 Section 1 are on file. IT Operations ships standard-issue laptops, docking stations, and peripherals via courier to your home address (for remote and hybrid employees) or holds them for pickup at your assigned office (Northlake, Austin, or Dublin) for employees who will be primarily on-site. Standard equipment ships seven to ten business days before your start date; employees who accept offers with less than two weeks' notice may receive a loaner laptop on day one while their permanent equipment ships separately.

Before equipment ships, you will receive an email from IT Operations containing your Contoso Corp network username and temporary password, along with instructions for activating multi-factor authentication on your personal phone or a company-provided token. Do not attempt to log into company systems from a personal device beyond the initial activation step; the full expectations for account use, device security, and data handling are governed by the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy, which you will be asked to formally acknowledge during your first week.

Your hiring manager will reach out directly, usually within the first few days after you accept your offer, to introduce themselves, share a rough outline of your first two weeks, and answer any informal questions that did not come up during the interview process. Many managers also use this window to schedule a brief welcome call so that you arrive on day one already having met at least one familiar face beyond HR and recruiting.

Approximately one week before your start date, you will be assigned an onboarding buddy: a peer, typically from your immediate team or a closely adjacent one, who is not your manager and whose role is to answer the small day-to-day questions that can feel awkward to ask a supervisor. Your buddy will usually send an introductory message before you start, and many buddies offer to have lunch or coffee with you during your first week.

If you are joining a team based in Northlake, Austin, or Dublin and will be working on-site or hybrid, you should also expect an email from Workplace Services with office-specific check-in instructions, parking or transit guidance, and a photo submission request for your building access badge. Employees who submit a photo before their start date typically have an active badge waiting for them at the front desk on day one rather than needing a temporary visitor pass.

Take time before your start date to review the offer letter, the summary of benefits eligibility timelines, and the digital new-hire packet sent by HR, which includes an overview of the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. You are not expected to read the full handbook before day one, but skimming the table of contents will help you recognize references to it during orientation and know where to look later when specific questions arise.

If anything in the pre-boarding checklist is unclear, incomplete, or delayed, your first point of contact is always the HR Onboarding team through the case-tracking tool inside the HR Portal, not your hiring manager. Routing questions through the correct channel keeps your paperwork moving even when your manager is traveling or otherwise unavailable in the days before you start.

Finally, plan your commute or travel for day one with a buffer. Orientation sessions begin promptly, and badge or laptop issues are far easier for the front-desk and IT teams to resolve if you arrive fifteen to twenty minutes before your scheduled start time rather than right at it.

Employees relocating from another country to join the Dublin office, or transferring internally from Northlake or Austin, should also work with HR's mobility team well before the pre-boarding checklist opens, since cross-border relocation logistics are handled on a separate timeline from the standard onboarding checklist and can take substantially longer to finalize than domestic paperwork.

If you are joining Contoso Corp with prior service, for example returning as a rehire within twelve months of a previous departure, let your recruiter know as early as possible. Rehires sometimes retain certain benefits eligibility timelines or accrued vacation balances from their earlier employment, and HR needs advance notice to configure the HR Portal correctly so those balances are not lost or delayed during pre-boarding.

Parents and caregivers who anticipate needing early accommodations, whether a modified start time, a lactation room reservation, or an ergonomic equipment request, are encouraged to raise these needs during pre-boarding rather than waiting until day one. Workplace Services and HR can typically have accommodations ready before you arrive if given at least five business days' notice.

## Your First Day

Day one begins at the front desk of your assigned office, or, for fully remote hires, with a scheduled video check-in with your onboarding buddy and an HR onboarding specialist. On-site new hires in Northlake, Austin, and Dublin all follow a similar check-in flow: present a government-issued photo ID at reception, confirm your identity against the visitor and new-hire list, and collect your building access badge, which security will activate on the spot if your photo was submitted in advance.

Each office has a dedicated new-hire welcome area near reception where a member of Workplace Services will greet you, provide a building map, and walk you through basic logistics: restrooms, the nearest kitchen or café, quiet rooms, printer locations, and emergency exits. Northlake's headquarters campus also includes a short shuttle route between buildings, which Workplace Services will explain if your team is not located in the main tower.

Your first stop after check-in is IT setup. A technician or a self-service kiosk, depending on office, will help you activate your laptop, confirm multi-factor authentication is working correctly, connect to the corporate network, and verify that your email, calendar, and core collaboration tools are provisioned and accessible. If you received a loaner laptop, IT will confirm the timeline for your permanent equipment and make sure your files and settings will transfer smoothly once it arrives.

Account provisioning on day one typically includes your Contoso Corp email address, calendar access, the internal knowledge base, the HR Portal employee self-service dashboard, and baseline access to whatever team-specific systems your manager requested during pre-boarding. Some systems, particularly those with elevated permissions such as financial reporting tools or production engineering environments, require a manager or system-owner approval step and may take an additional one to three business days to activate; this is expected and not a sign that anything was missed.

Around mid-morning, most new hires attend a company-wide orientation session, held in person at each office and streamed for remote attendees, that introduces Contoso Corp's history, mission, organizational structure, and executive leadership team. This session also covers the basics of how the company is organized across Northlake, Austin, and Dublin, and gives a short overview of major internal systems you will use regularly, including the HR Portal, the expense reporting tool, and the internal knowledge base.

Your manager will personally greet you at some point on day one, whether in person or by video call, to formally welcome you to the team. This welcome typically includes introductions to immediate teammates, a walkthrough of the team's current priorities, and a preview of the meetings and one-on-ones you can expect over the coming weeks. Many managers also use this time to talk informally about working styles and communication preferences so that the relationship starts on clear footing.

Your onboarding buddy will typically connect with you in person or virtually by early afternoon on day one. Unlike your manager, your buddy is there purely to help you get oriented socially and practically: where people tend to eat lunch, which internal chat channels are useful to join, how the team runs its stand-ups, and any unwritten norms that are useful to know but rarely show up in formal documentation.

Day one also includes a short session, often self-paced through the learning management system, introducing the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. You are not expected to complete every required training module on day one; the handbook overview simply orients you to where policies live, how the introductory period works, and who to contact for common HR questions, setting the stage for the more detailed compliance training that begins in earnest during your first week.

Toward the end of day one, HR will confirm that your I-9, tax withholding, and direct deposit paperwork are all fully processed, and will flag anything still outstanding so it can be resolved quickly. If your background check has not yet cleared by day one, which does occasionally happen for roles requiring supplemental screening, you may begin working in a limited access status while final clearance is completed.

Remote new hires follow largely the same structure but on a compressed video schedule: a morning IT and account-verification call, a mid-morning virtual orientation session streamed from Northlake, an early-afternoon welcome call with their manager, and an end-of-day check-in with their onboarding buddy. Equipment that has not yet arrived by day one for remote hires is tracked by IT Operations, who will proactively reach out with updated shipping status rather than leaving the new hire to follow up.

By the end of day one, most new hires have an active badge or verified remote access, a working laptop, core system accounts provisioned, a completed welcome conversation with their manager, and an introduction to their onboarding buddy. If any of these pieces are missing by end of day, HR Onboarding wants to hear about it immediately through the HR Portal case tool so it does not compound into a slower first week.

Lunch on day one is typically informal and team-organized rather than centrally scheduled. Many managers ask a teammate or the onboarding buddy to take the new hire to a nearby restaurant or the office cafeteria, where available; in Northlake, the headquarters campus cafeteria offers a complimentary meal voucher for every employee's first day, while Austin and Dublin employees are usually reimbursed for a modest first-day lunch through the standard expense tool.

Late in the afternoon, most new hires receive a short digital survey from HR Onboarding asking how the day went: whether equipment and access worked as expected, whether the welcome from their manager and buddy felt sufficient, and whether anything logistical fell through the cracks. This feedback loop is reviewed weekly by the HR Onboarding team and is one of the primary ways the onboarding program itself gets improved over time.

If your start date happens to fall on a company holiday observed by your office location or during a scheduled all-hands or town hall event, your manager and HR will have already adjusted your schedule during pre-boarding so that day one still includes the core check-in, IT setup, and welcome elements described above, simply shifted to accommodate the broader company event.

## Your First Week

Your first week is structured around a blend of required compliance training, team integration, and the beginning of real project work, roughly in that order of priority. HR schedules most required training modules for completion within your first five business days, though a small number of specialized modules tied to specific roles may extend into week two.

The first major training module is unconscious bias awareness, drawn directly from the Contoso Corp Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Policy. This interactive module, delivered through the learning management system, walks through common forms of unconscious bias in hiring, performance evaluation, and day-to-day collaboration, and asks you to complete a short reflective exercise at the end. All employees, regardless of level or department, are required to complete this module within their first week and to retake a refresher version annually.

The second required module covers information security and acceptable use, based on the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy. This training covers phishing recognition, password and multi-factor authentication requirements, acceptable use of company devices and cloud storage, data classification basics, and the process for reporting a suspected security incident. Completion of this module is a prerequisite for retaining access to several internal systems, so new hires who do not finish it within the first week may find certain accounts automatically restricted until it is done.

You will also complete a short workplace conduct and anti-harassment training module, which reinforces the reporting channels and expectations described in the broader Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. This module is intentionally brief for new hires, since it overlaps with content covered during the orientation session on day one, but completion is still tracked and required.

Midway through your first week, HR will send a personalized benefits enrollment packet referencing the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide. Newly hired employees have a 30-calendar-day window from their start date to elect medical, dental, and vision coverage, enroll in flexible spending or health savings accounts where eligible, and designate life insurance beneficiaries. Coverage elected during this initial window becomes effective on the first of the month following 30 days of employment, and if no election is made within the window, you will default to the guide's baseline coverage tier until the next annual open enrollment period.

Because benefits decisions can be complex, HR schedules an optional but strongly recommended thirty-minute benefits orientation call during week one, where a benefits specialist walks through plan options, cost differences between coverage tiers, and how the Health and Welfare Benefits Guide's dependent eligibility rules work for employees who plan to add a spouse, domestic partner, or children to their plan.

On the team side, your manager will hold a structured one-on-one sometime during your first week, separate from the day-one welcome conversation, to go deeper on your role's core responsibilities, the tools and systems specific to your function, and the shape of your first thirty days. This is also typically when your manager shares any team-specific documentation, style guides, or codebases that were not covered during general orientation.

Your onboarding buddy remains your first call for informal questions throughout the week: how to book a conference room, which recurring meetings you should join versus simply have visibility into, how expense reports actually get approved in practice, and similar day-to-day logistics that are faster to ask a peer than to look up. Most buddy relationships continue informally for the first 60 to 90 days, well past the point where the assignment technically ends.

You will typically shadow at least one recurring team meeting, one cross-functional meeting involving a partner team, and, where applicable, a customer or stakeholder-facing call during your first week. The goal is exposure rather than participation; you are not expected to contribute substantively yet, only to start building context for how the team's work connects to the wider organization.

By the end of week one, most new hires have completed all three required compliance training modules, initiated their benefits elections, had a substantive one-on-one with their manager, and begun light contribution to at least one real piece of work, even if it is small in scope. If any required training remains incomplete due to scheduling conflicts, flag it to your manager and to HR Onboarding immediately rather than letting it slip into week two unaddressed.

Employees joining the Dublin office should also note that a small number of country-specific compliance modules, covering local employment law topics not addressed in the general handbook, are assigned during week one in addition to the standard global curriculum. HR will indicate clearly in the learning management system which modules are globally required versus location-specific.

If, by the end of your first week, you still lack access to a system you need for your role, have not received a piece of promised equipment, or have unresolved payroll or benefits questions, escalate through the HR Portal case tool and copy your manager. Waiting quietly rarely resolves these issues faster, and Contoso Corp's onboarding teams would much rather hear about a problem in week one than discover it during a thirty-day check-in.

Employees in customer-facing, financial, or engineering roles with production system access will also be assigned role-specific compliance training during week one, layered on top of the three universal modules. Examples include a data privacy handling module for anyone touching customer records, and a secure coding practices module for engineers with commit access to production repositories, both of which are tracked separately in the learning management system from the general onboarding curriculum.

Toward the end of the first week, most managers schedule a short informal retrospective, sometimes over coffee or a walk rather than a formal meeting, to ask how the week felt overall: what was clear, what was confusing, and what the new hire wishes they had known on day one. This conversation feeds directly into how that manager runs onboarding for the next new hire on the team.

New hires should also use the first week to introduce themselves proactively in relevant internal chat channels, subscribe to the internal newsletter distributed by Corporate Communications, and add themselves to the internal directory with a current photo and a short bio, all of which make it easier for colleagues across Northlake, Austin, and Dublin to recognize and connect with you as you begin working across teams.

## 30-60-90 Day Milestones

Contoso Corp structures the early months of employment around a 30-60-90 day framework, which maps directly onto the introductory period described in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. The introductory period lasts the first ninety calendar days of employment and exists to give both the new employee and Contoso Corp a structured opportunity to confirm fit, surface early concerns, and course-correct quickly rather than waiting for a full annual review cycle.

The 30-day milestone check-in is primarily about foundational orientation. Your manager will confirm that all required compliance training from your first week is fully complete, that your benefits elections were submitted within the enrollment window described in the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide, and that you have reliable access to every system your role requires. This conversation is deliberately low-pressure and focused on removing blockers rather than evaluating performance.

At the 30-day mark, you and your manager will also draft an initial set of goals for your first ninety days, using the goal-setting framework described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy. These early goals are typically narrower and more concrete than the annual objectives set later in the year; they might include completing a specific onboarding project, achieving proficiency in a core internal tool, or building working relationships with a defined set of cross-functional partners.

The 60-day milestone check-in shifts focus toward early contribution and integration. Your manager will ask about the quality and pace of your ramp-up, whether the initial 30-day goals are on track, and whether any additional training, mentorship, or resources would help close remaining gaps. This is also a natural point for your manager to solicit informal feedback from teammates who have worked with you directly during your first two months.

By 60 days, most new hires have moved from observing meetings to actively contributing within them, have taken ownership of at least one defined piece of work, and have a clear enough picture of team dynamics to identify who to go to for different kinds of questions without relying heavily on their onboarding buddy. If progress feels slower than this, that is useful information for the 60-day conversation rather than a cause for alarm on its own.

The 90-day milestone check-in is the most formal of the three and directly closes out the introductory period defined in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. Your manager completes a structured introductory-period review that evaluates performance against the goals set at the 30-day mark, documents progress in the systems accessible through the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, and confirms your continued employment status.

For the great majority of new hires, the 90-day review results in straightforward confirmation that the introductory period has been successfully completed, with no change to employment status. In cases where performance concerns have emerged, the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook allows for an extension of the introductory period by up to thirty additional days, or, in rare cases, separation during the introductory period, both of which would be discussed directly and transparently with you well before the 90-day date arrives rather than as a surprise.

The 90-day check-in is also when your manager will help you set your first full-cycle performance goals under the standard rhythm described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, transitioning you from onboarding-specific objectives to the same goal-setting and review cadence followed by the rest of your team.

Throughout the 30-60-90 day period, your access to internal systems, your training completions, and your benefits status are all visible to you directly through the HR Portal employee self-service dashboard, so you do not need to wait for a formal check-in to confirm that something is on track. Reviewing this dashboard periodically during your first ninety days is a good habit that many long-tenured employees continue well beyond onboarding.

Your onboarding buddy relationship formally winds down around the 90-day mark, though many new hires and their buddies continue meeting informally afterward. If your assigned buddy changes roles or leaves the team before your 90-day milestone, HR Onboarding will assign a replacement promptly so you are not left without an informal point of contact during this period.

If you have questions at any point during your first ninety days that are not resolved by your manager, your onboarding buddy, or the HR Portal knowledge base, HR Onboarding remains available through the case-tracking tool, and Employee Relations is available for any concern you would prefer to raise outside your direct management chain. Both channels are described in more detail in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook, and using them early is always preferable to letting a concern linger unaddressed into your next review cycle.

Ultimately, the 30-60-90 day framework exists to make the first three months at Contoso Corp predictable rather than ambiguous: you should always know what is expected of you next, where to find the policy or system that governs a given question, and who on the team is available to help you get there.

Managers are expected to document each of the three milestone check-ins in the HR Portal, not merely discuss them verbally, so that there is a clear written record supporting the 90-day introductory-period review required under the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. New hires can view their manager's documented notes from each milestone at any time through the employee self-service dashboard.

New hires who joined through an internal transfer rather than external hire follow a condensed version of the 30-60-90 day framework, since compliance training and benefits elections typically carry over from their prior role. Internal transfers still receive a 30-day and 90-day check-in focused on goal-setting and integration into the new team, but skip most of the paperwork-oriented elements described earlier in this guide.

Employees who began their Contoso Corp career through a contractor-to-full-time conversion should note that their introductory period, and therefore the 30-60-90 day framework, begins on their full-time conversion date rather than their original contractor start date, consistent with the definition used in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook.
