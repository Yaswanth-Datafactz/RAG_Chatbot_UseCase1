# Contoso Corp Remote Work and Flexible Work Arrangements Policy

## Eligibility for Remote Work

Contoso Corp supports a range of work location arrangements because our teams span Northlake, Austin, and Dublin and because the nature of individual roles varies widely in how much they depend on physical presence. This policy defines three location categories that every position at Contoso Corp is assigned: remote-eligible, hybrid, and on-site required. Employees should confirm their assigned category with their manager and in Workday under the Position Details tab, since the category determines which sections of this policy apply to them and what obligations follow from the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook regarding attendance and availability.

Remote-eligible roles are positions whose core responsibilities can be performed effectively from a location other than a Contoso Corp office on an ongoing basis. Historically this category has included most software engineering, data science, technical writing, and individual-contributor product management roles, as well as specialist functions such as security engineering and corporate finance analysis where the work product is primarily digital and collaboration can occur asynchronously or over video. A role's remote-eligible designation is set by the People Team in partnership with the hiring manager at the time the requisition is created and is reviewed annually during the workforce planning cycle described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy.

Hybrid roles require a regular in-office presence but do not require daily attendance. Most people-manager roles at the manager and senior manager levels, along with roles that depend on frequent in-person collaboration such as recruiting, executive support, and certain marketing and design functions, are designated hybrid. Employees in hybrid roles are assigned a home office, meaning the Northlake headquarters, the Austin office, or the Dublin office, and their in-office expectations are measured against that home office regardless of how far they choose to live from it.

On-site required roles are those where the work cannot reasonably be performed away from a Contoso Corp facility, including facilities and workplace operations staff, IT hardware support technicians, on-site security personnel, and lab-based hardware engineering roles that depend on equipment housed at Northlake. Employees in on-site required roles are not eligible for the remote or hybrid provisions of this policy, although they remain eligible for the occasional flexible-day accommodations described later in this section, subject to manager approval and operational need.

New hires are informed of their role's location category in their offer letter, and any change to that category after hire, whether requested by the employee or proposed by the business, must go through the flexible work arrangement request process described below rather than being agreed informally between employee and manager. This ensures the change is properly reflected in Workday, in payroll tax withholding, and in the employee's applicable state or country employment terms.

Employees who wish to move from a hybrid or on-site required designation to remote-eligible status must submit a Flexible Work Arrangement Request through the People Team portal at least six weeks before the desired effective date. The request requires manager endorsement, confirmation from the employee's functional VP that the change does not create a coverage gap on the team, and a review by the People Team for compliance with tax residency and right-to-work considerations. Requests are evaluated on a case-by-case basis and are not guaranteed regardless of tenure or performance rating.

Employees relocating to a new city, state, or country must notify the People Team before the move, even if their role is already remote-eligible, because a change of work location can trigger new state tax registration, new benefits eligibility rules, or, for international moves, a determination of whether Contoso Corp is able to legally employ the individual in the destination country at all. Employees who relocate without prior notification may be required to return to their prior location at their own expense or may face a pause in payroll processing until the new location is validated.

Remote-eligible employees are expected to maintain a primary residence within a country where Contoso Corp has an established legal entity or an approved employer-of-record partnership. As of this policy's effective date those countries are the United States and Ireland. Employees who wish to work from any other country, including on a temporary basis of more than fifteen consecutive business days, must obtain advance written approval from the People Team and Legal, because uncoordinated cross-border work can create unplanned corporate tax presence, immigration violations, or data protection exposure under regimes such as GDPR.

Short-term remote work from a non-home-country location, sometimes called workation, is permitted for up to fifteen business days per calendar year for remote-eligible and hybrid employees, subject to manager approval submitted at least two weeks in advance through the People Team portal. Employees traveling internationally for this purpose remain responsible for reviewing visa and customs requirements themselves; Contoso Corp does not sponsor visas for personal travel and does not reimburse related costs, which is a distinct scenario from business travel covered under the Contoso Corp Business Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy.

Contractors and other non-employee workers engaged through staffing agencies are not covered by this policy. Their work location terms are governed by the applicable statement of work and the agency agreement, and any location flexibility for contractors must be negotiated through Procurement rather than through the People Team portal used by employees.

Interns and new graduates hired into the annual rotational program are, for their first six months, designated hybrid regardless of the underlying role's general category, in order to support onboarding, mentorship, and early skill development. After the initial six months, an intern or new graduate converting to a permanent position is reassessed against the standard eligibility criteria described above.

Employees with a documented disability who require a location or schedule accommodation that differs from their role's assigned category should route their request through the accommodations process rather than the standard Flexible Work Arrangement Request, so that it can be evaluated under applicable disability accommodation law with the appropriate confidentiality protections, as referenced in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook.

This policy does not create an entitlement to any particular work location and does not modify the at-will nature of employment described in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. Contoso Corp reserves the right to change a role's location category, including converting a remote-eligible role to hybrid, where business needs require it, with reasonable notice to affected employees of no less than sixty days except in circumstances of urgent operational need.

Managers considering a Flexible Work Arrangement Request are expected to evaluate it against consistent, documented criteria rather than personal preference, including the requesting employee's recent performance history under the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, whether the role's actual day-to-day work has shifted meaningfully toward asynchronous, digitally deliverable output, and whether granting the request would leave the team without adequate in-person coverage for training, client-facing work, or cross-functional collaboration that the team still depends on.

Denied Flexible Work Arrangement Requests must include a brief written explanation from the manager, retained by the People Team, so that the employee understands the specific reasoning and so that similar future requests can be evaluated consistently across the organization. An employee who believes a request was denied for reasons inconsistent with this policy may ask the People Team to review the decision independently of the original manager.

## Hybrid Work Expectations

Employees designated hybrid are expected to be present at their home office location a minimum number of days each week, measured on a rolling four-week average rather than a rigid weekly count, to allow reasonable flexibility around travel, appointments, and occasional personal circumstances. The minimum is three in-office days per week at the Northlake and Austin offices and two in-office days per week at the Dublin office, reflecting differences in team density and typical commute patterns across those locations.

Managers designate which weekdays are anchor days for their team, and employees are expected to be present on those specific days rather than choosing days independently, so that in-person time reliably overlaps across the team. Anchor days are published on the team's shared calendar and reviewed at least once per quarter; a manager who wishes to change anchor days must give the team at least two weeks' notice.

Attendance for hybrid roles is tracked through badge access data at Northlake and Austin and through a shared sign-in sheet at the smaller Dublin office, and is reviewed by managers as part of normal performance conversations rather than through automated enforcement. Falling meaningfully short of the minimum on a sustained basis, absent an approved exception, is treated as a performance and conduct matter subject to the standard processes in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy.

Hybrid employees may request a temporary reduction in in-office days for a defined period, such as during a family medical situation or a short-term move, by discussing the request with their manager and, where the reduction exceeds four consecutive weeks, obtaining sign-off from the People Team so the exception is documented consistently across teams.

On days when a hybrid employee is scheduled to work from the office but a legitimate conflict arises, such as a sudden illness, a home repair emergency, or inclement weather that makes travel unsafe, the employee should simply message their manager as they would for any other unplanned absence; occasional one-off exceptions do not require a formal request and are not counted against the rolling four-week average.

Desk arrangements at Northlake and Austin follow a neighborhood-based hoteling model: employees do not have permanently assigned desks but reserve a desk within their team's designated floor neighborhood through the Contoso Corp workplace app before arriving. Dublin, being a smaller office, retains assigned desks for now given lower headcount density, though this is expected to move to the same hoteling model if Dublin headcount grows past two hundred employees.

Hybrid employees are expected to treat in-office days as genuinely collaborative time rather than as a change of scenery for solo focus work. Managers are encouraged to schedule team meetings, one-on-ones, design reviews, and onboarding sessions on anchor days specifically so that the in-person time produces the interaction that distinguishes it from a remote workday.

Meeting etiquette on hybrid days requires that when any participant in a meeting is joining remotely, all participants, including those physically present in a conference room, join the video call individually from their own laptop rather than clustering around a single room camera, unless the meeting is explicitly designated as an all-hands or town hall format. This ensures remote participants can see and hear everyone equally rather than experiencing a degraded, room-camera view of the discussion.

Hybrid employees traveling between Contoso Corp offices, for example an Austin-based employee attending a planning session at Northlake, should book travel through the process described in the Contoso Corp Business Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy; such trips do not count against the employee's personal workation allowance and are treated as ordinary business travel.

New hires into hybrid roles are expected to meet the full in-office minimum starting in their first week, without a grace period, because early in-person exposure to the team materially improves onboarding outcomes; managers are expected to plan onboarding schedules accordingly and to front-load in-person orientation activities during the new hire's first month.

Hybrid employees relocating within commuting distance of a different Contoso Corp office than the one to which they are currently assigned, for instance moving from the Austin metro area within range of a future satellite office, should notify the People Team so their home office assignment can be updated; continuing to badge into a different office than the one on record for more than thirty days requires an updated Flexible Work Arrangement Request.

Team leads are expected to publish a rolling two-week look-ahead of who will be in the office on which days, using the shared team calendar, so that colleagues who depend on cross-functional collaboration, such as partner teams in Legal, Finance, or Marketing, can plan joint in-person sessions efficiently rather than guessing at availability.

Exceptions to the standard three-day and two-day minimums may be granted at the functional VP level for specific teams whose work genuinely benefits from a different cadence, such as a team running a temporary in-person product sprint that requires four or five days per week for a defined period, or a support rotation that benefits from a lighter one-day minimum during a quiet season; any such exception must be documented with the People Team and has a defined end date.

Employees who have been designated hybrid but whose in-office presence consistently falls below the rolling four-week average without an approved exception will first receive an informal reminder from their manager, followed by a documented conversation if the pattern continues into a second consecutive four-week period. Only after these lower-level conversations have failed to resolve the pattern will the matter be escalated into the formal performance process described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, consistent with Contoso Corp's general preference for coaching before formal discipline.

Visiting employees who travel to a Contoso Corp office other than their assigned home office, whether for a specific project, a team offsite, or simply to spend time with colleagues, should reserve a visitor desk through the workplace app in advance so that facilities staff can plan seating capacity; visiting a different office does not count toward or against the visiting employee's own home office attendance minimum for that week.

## Home Office Equipment and Stipend

Contoso Corp provides a one-time home office setup stipend of one thousand two hundred dollars to every employee newly designated remote-eligible or hybrid, whether at hire or upon a later change in designation, to help establish a functional and ergonomically sound home workspace. The stipend is administered through the standard expense reimbursement system referenced in the Contoso Corp Business Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy and must be claimed within ninety days of eligibility to avoid forfeiture.

Eligible purchases under the stipend include a height-adjustable or fixed-height desk, an ergonomic office chair, an external monitor up to twenty-seven inches, a monitor arm or stand, an external keyboard and mouse, a laptop stand or docking station, and supplementary lighting or a headset suitable for video calls. Purchases of general home furnishings, decorative items, or equipment intended for uses other than Contoso Corp work are not reimbursable under this stipend.

In addition to the one-time stipend, remote-eligible and hybrid employees receive an ongoing monthly connectivity allowance of fifty dollars, paid through payroll rather than expense reimbursement, intended to offset the cost of home internet service. This allowance is treated as taxable income in jurisdictions where local tax law requires it, and Payroll will apply the appropriate withholding automatically; employees do not need to submit separate documentation to receive it.

Employees who prefer not to purchase equipment personally may instead request that Contoso Corp ship standard-issue equipment directly, consisting of a company-selected monitor, keyboard, mouse, and laptop stand, through the IT Asset Management team. Equipment provided this way remains Contoso Corp property and is tracked by asset tag in the same inventory system used for office equipment, whereas equipment purchased through the stipend and expense process becomes the employee's personal property.

All remote-eligible and hybrid employees are eligible for a complimentary ergonomic self-assessment, delivered through a short online questionnaire administered by Contoso Corp's occupational health vendor, and may request a live video consultation with an ergonomics specialist if the self-assessment or the employee's own comfort concerns warrant it. Employees experiencing pain or discomfort they believe is related to their home workstation should raise it promptly with the People Team rather than waiting for a scheduled review, so that adjustments can be made before the issue becomes a repetitive strain injury.

Company-issued laptops and any other Contoso Corp-owned equipment used at home remain subject to the same security, patching, and acceptable-use requirements that apply to equipment used in a Contoso Corp office, as described in the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy. Employees may not install unauthorized software, disable endpoint security agents, or permit household members to use a company-issued device.

Home network security is a shared responsibility. Remote-eligible and hybrid employees are required to secure their home Wi-Fi network with WPA2 or WPA3 encryption and a unique password rather than a router's default credentials, to keep router firmware reasonably current, and to avoid connecting Contoso Corp equipment to unsecured public networks without first enabling the company VPN, consistent with the baseline controls in the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy.

Employees are not required to purchase a dedicated business-grade internet plan, but connectivity that cannot reliably support video calls, screen sharing, and standard collaboration tools is considered the employee's responsibility to resolve; chronic connectivity problems that affect an employee's ability to meet job expectations may be addressed through a performance conversation with their manager if left unresolved after reasonable support from IT.

Equipment purchased through the stipend or shipped by IT Asset Management must be reasonably protected from damage, loss, and theft, and employees should notify IT Asset Management promptly if company-owned equipment is lost, stolen, or damaged, so it can be deactivated or replaced and, where relevant, so that Information Security can assess any data exposure risk under the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy.

Upon separation from Contoso Corp, whether voluntary or involuntary, employees must return all company-owned equipment, including laptops, monitors, docking stations, and any other assets tracked under an asset tag, within ten business days of the separation date. IT Asset Management will provide a prepaid shipping label and packing instructions; equipment not returned within this window may result in the equipment's replacement cost being deducted from final pay to the extent permitted by applicable state or country law.

Equipment purchased by the employee personally using the one-time stipend does not need to be returned upon separation, since it is the employee's property, but any Contoso Corp software, VPN credentials, or access profiles installed on personally owned devices used for work must be removed as part of the standard offboarding checklist administered by IT.

Employees moving between hybrid and remote-eligible designations, or between home offices, do not receive an additional home office stipend unless more than twenty-four months have passed since their last stipend was issued, in which case they become eligible again to account for equipment wear and changing workspace needs.

Requests for equipment beyond the standard eligible list, such as a second external monitor, a specialized ergonomic keyboard, or adaptive equipment related to a documented disability, should be routed through the accommodations process or, for non-disability-related requests, submitted to the employee's manager for discretionary approval, since such requests fall outside the standard stipend program described in this section.

Employees based in Ireland receive the home office stipend and monthly connectivity allowance in euros at the prevailing exchange rate published by Payroll on the first business day of the month in which the stipend is claimed, and any tax treatment specific to Irish payroll, including how the connectivity allowance is reported under Irish Revenue rules, is handled automatically by the Dublin payroll team without requiring the employee to take separate action.

Damaged or malfunctioning company-issued equipment should be reported to IT Asset Management rather than repaired independently, even for something as simple as a swollen laptop battery or a failing monitor cable, both because independent repairs can void manufacturer warranties that Contoso Corp relies on and because some failure modes, particularly with batteries, present a genuine safety risk that Contoso Corp's vendor relationships are better equipped to handle safely.

## Core Collaboration Hours

Because Contoso Corp employees are distributed across Pacific Time in Northlake, Central Time in Austin, and Irish Time in Dublin, this policy establishes core collaboration hours during which all remote-eligible and hybrid employees are expected to be reachable and available for synchronous meetings, regardless of where they are physically working on a given day. Core collaboration hours are nine a.m. to twelve p.m. Central Time, which corresponds to seven a.m. to ten a.m. Pacific Time and three p.m. to six p.m. Irish Time for most of the year, adjusting automatically as each region's local clocks shift for daylight saving or summer time.

Outside of core collaboration hours, employees are expected to structure the remainder of their working day flexibly around their own personal schedules, provided they complete their standard hours and remain responsive to reasonably time-sensitive messages within their own working day. Managers should not schedule recurring meetings outside core collaboration hours without a specific, documented reason, since doing so disproportionately burdens employees in the region for whom the meeting falls outside a normal working day.

Individual teams may adopt a narrower or shifted core collaboration window than the company-wide default described above if the team's specific composition of time zones makes a different window more workable, but any such team-level adjustment must still fall within the broader nine a.m. to six p.m. local working day for every included time zone and must be documented on the team's shared working agreement so that partner teams and new hires can find it easily.

Video-on is the expected default for internal meetings during core collaboration hours, particularly for one-on-ones, team stand-ups, design reviews, and any meeting with five or fewer participants, because visual presence materially improves communication quality in a distributed team and helps managers and peers notice signs of disengagement or difficulty that would otherwise go unnoticed. Employees may turn video off for large all-hands meetings, for calls taken while walking or commuting with prior notice to the meeting organizer, or when a specific personal circumstance warrants it on a given day.

Employees who need to keep video off on a recurring basis due to a medical condition, a caregiving situation, home circumstances, or any other ongoing reason should discuss it with their manager so a standing understanding can be reached; this is not something an employee needs to justify meeting by meeting once a manager is aware of the underlying reason.

Meeting organizers are expected to default to asynchronous methods, such as a written proposal circulated for comment or a recorded walkthrough, whenever a topic does not genuinely require real-time discussion, and to reserve synchronous meetings during core collaboration hours for decisions, brainstorming, and relationship-building activities that benefit from live interaction. This expectation exists specifically to protect focus time and to avoid overloading the narrow window during which all three regional offices overlap.

Recurring cross-regional meetings, such as weekly leadership syncs or quarterly planning sessions that involve participants from Northlake, Austin, and Dublin together, must be scheduled within core collaboration hours by default. A meeting organizer who believes a specific recurring meeting cannot reasonably fit within core collaboration hours must obtain sign-off from the functional VPs of every region involved and must rotate the meeting time across a fair pattern so that no single region permanently bears an inconvenient time slot.

Employees are expected to keep their working hours and time zone current in their Workday profile and in their calendar tool, so that colleagues scheduling meetings can see accurate availability rather than defaulting to their own local assumptions. Calendar tools automatically display meeting times in each invitee's local time zone, and employees are responsible for double-checking the displayed time before accepting an invitation that appears to fall outside core collaboration hours.

Managers of distributed teams are expected to rotate the burden of any necessarily inconvenient recurring meeting, such as a global planning call that cannot avoid falling early morning for one region, so that the same individuals are not consistently asked to accommodate the other regions' preferred hours. Managers should document the rotation schedule and review it at least twice a year for fairness as team composition changes.

Employees traveling for personal reasons across time zones, including during an approved workation under the eligibility section of this policy, remain responsible for attending meetings scheduled during their home time zone's core collaboration hours unless they have arranged in advance with their manager to shift their schedule; personal travel is not, by itself, a basis for missing standing team commitments.

Performance expectations under this policy are explicitly location-agnostic: employees are evaluated on the outcomes and quality of their work as described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, not on which office they worked from, how many hours they were visibly online, or whether they kept video on for every call. Managers are expected to set clear, outcome-based goals for remote and hybrid employees precisely so that evaluation does not inadvertently reward visibility over impact.

Where a manager has concerns about a remote or hybrid employee's responsiveness, availability during core collaboration hours, or overall performance, those concerns should be raised directly with the employee as a normal coaching conversation in the first instance, following the same standards that would apply to an on-site employee, and escalated through the formal performance process described in the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy only if the concerns persist after that conversation.

This policy will be reviewed at least annually by the People Team in consultation with regional leadership in Northlake, Austin, and Dublin, to confirm that the defined core collaboration hours, stipend amounts, and hybrid attendance minimums continue to reflect the company's actual distribution of employees and evolving best practice; material changes will be communicated to all employees at least thirty days before taking effect.

New employees and any employee transferring into a distributed team for the first time are introduced to the core collaboration hours concept during onboarding, and managers are expected to walk new team members through the team's specific working agreement, including any team-level adjustment to the default window, within the first two weeks so that scheduling expectations are clear before the new employee begins accepting meeting invitations independently.

Employees who are unsure whether a specific meeting falls within core collaboration hours, particularly around daylight saving transitions when Northlake and Austin shift a week or more apart from Dublin's clock change under European rules, should confirm the meeting time directly with the organizer rather than assuming; the People Team publishes an updated core collaboration hours reference chart each time a regional clock change is about to create a temporary misalignment.
