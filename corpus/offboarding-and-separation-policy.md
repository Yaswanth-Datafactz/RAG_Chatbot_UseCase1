# Contoso Corp Offboarding and Separation Policy

## Types of Separation

Contoso Corp recognizes that employment relationships end for many different reasons, and the process an employee experiences on the way out the door should match the circumstances of that departure. This policy describes the standard offboarding process that applies across Northlake, Austin, and Dublin, and it works alongside the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy, the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide, the Contoso Corp Retirement Savings Plan Guide, the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy, and the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy. Human Resources is the single point of coordination for every separation, regardless of type, and no manager should attempt to process a departure without looping in an HR business partner first.

Voluntary resignation occurs when an employee chooses to end their employment, whether to accept another opportunity, relocate, retire, or for personal reasons they are not obligated to disclose. Employees resigning in good standing who provide the recommended notice period, complete their assigned handoff duties, and return company property on schedule are eligible for rehire consideration and generally receive the most favorable treatment under this policy, including a neutral or positive reference from HR upon request.

Involuntary termination covers separations initiated by Contoso Corp for cause, including but not limited to serious policy violations, sustained performance deficiencies documented under the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, violations of the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy, or conduct that breaches the Contoso Corp Code of Conduct. Terminations for cause are reviewed by HR and, where warranted, Legal before they are finalized, and the employee's manager must have documented at least one prior corrective conversation unless the conduct in question is a summary-dismissal offense such as theft, workplace violence, or falsification of records.

Layoff, also referred to internally as a reduction in force or RIF, occurs when Contoso Corp eliminates a position or group of positions for business reasons unrelated to individual performance, such as restructuring, a discontinued product line, or a cost-reduction initiative approved by the executive leadership team. Employees separated through a layoff are not being separated for cause, remain eligible for rehire, and receive severance consideration as described later in this policy, along with outplacement support coordinated through HR where the size of the affected group warrants it.

Retirement is treated as a distinct category of voluntary separation for employees who intend to permanently exit the workforce rather than seek new employment elsewhere. Employees planning to retire are asked to provide at least sixty days of advance notice where feasible, meaningfully longer than the standard two-week resignation notice, so that HR can walk them through pension or 401(k) distribution timing under the Contoso Corp Retirement Savings Plan Guide, Medicare coordination, and any applicable retiree health arrangements described in the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide.

End of temporary assignment applies to fixed-term employees, interns, and contractors whose engagement concludes on a pre-agreed date or upon completion of a defined project scope. Because these separations are anticipated from the start of the assignment, no resignation notice is required, though HR still conducts standard offboarding steps including equipment return and account deactivation. Temporary employees who are subsequently converted to regular full-time or part-time status are not considered separated and do not go through this process at the point of conversion.

Mutual separation agreements are used in limited circumstances where Contoso Corp and an employee agree, by written agreement negotiated with HR and, for senior roles, Legal, that ending the employment relationship on specific terms serves both parties' interests. These agreements typically specify a separation date, any negotiated severance, confidentiality and non-disparagement terms, and are documented outside the standard involuntary-termination or resignation paperwork, though all standard offboarding logistics described in this policy still apply.

Job abandonment is treated as a voluntary resignation without notice. An employee who is absent without approved leave and without any communication to their manager or HR for three consecutive scheduled workdays is presumed to have voluntarily resigned, effective the last day worked, unless the absence is later shown to be connected to a qualifying leave under the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy or a legally protected absence the employee could not reasonably report in advance.

Death of an employee is administered by HR as a separation effective the date of death, with all final pay, benefits, and retirement account matters directed to the employee's designated beneficiary or estate in accordance with applicable state law. HR proactively reaches out to the emergency contact on file to coordinate next steps, including any life insurance claims available under the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide, and no equipment-return deadline is enforced against the family.

Regardless of separation type, every departing employee's manager and HR business partner jointly complete an offboarding checklist that captures the separation category, last day worked, notice period provided, rehire eligibility determination, and confirmation that each step in this policy, from final pay through exit interview, has been completed and documented in the HR information system.

Contoso Corp distinguishes these categories carefully because the separation reason drives downstream decisions in payroll, benefits, and future hiring, and an incorrectly recorded category can cause real problems for a former employee, such as an inaccurate unemployment filing or an unwarranted rehire restriction. HR business partners are trained to confirm the correct category with the manager before closing out a separation record, and any disputed categorization is escalated to the HR director for the affected office.

## Resignation Notice Period

Contoso Corp asks employees who are voluntarily resigning to provide at least two full weeks of written notice to their manager and to HR. Two weeks is a recommendation, not a legal requirement, and Contoso Corp does not condition an employee's final pay, benefits, or reference eligibility on providing notice, but the length and professionalism of the notice period is a factor HR and the manager may consider in the rehire eligibility determination described in the Types of Separation section above.

Notice should be submitted in writing, whether by email to the employee's manager and HR business partner or through the resignation workflow in the HR information system, and should state the intended last day worked. Verbal notice is acknowledged but Contoso Corp will ask the employee to confirm the resignation and last day in writing within twenty-four hours so that payroll, benefits, and IT offboarding timelines can be calculated accurately.

Employees in individual contributor roles are asked for the standard two weeks. Employees in people-manager, director, and above roles, or in roles designated as business-critical by their department head, are encouraged to provide three to four weeks where feasible, given the additional handoff and transition planning those roles typically require, though this remains a request rather than a condition of good standing.

If an employee provides less than the recommended two weeks, or resigns effective immediately, the manager and HR will assess whether the employee's continued presence for the remainder of the notice period is operationally desirable. Contoso Corp reserves the right to accept a resignation effective immediately, or on any date prior to the employee's proposed last day, and to relieve the employee of duties earlier than requested; in all such cases the employee is paid through the originally scheduled last day worked at their base rate, consistent with applicable state final-pay laws.

When Contoso Corp exercises its right to shorten a resignation notice period, this action is treated as an administrative decision and is not recorded as an involuntary termination for the employee's personnel file, unemployment filings, or rehire eligibility purposes. The employee's official separation reason remains voluntary resignation, and their reference will reflect that they resigned rather than were terminated.

During an active notice period, employees are expected to maintain normal performance standards, complete a documented handoff of open work, and continue to comply with all Contoso Corp policies, including the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy. HR and the manager may ask the departing employee to prepare transition documentation, train a successor or interim owner, and update status on active projects as part of a standard offboarding plan template maintained by each department.

Employees who wish to rescind a resignation before their last day worked should submit the request in writing to their manager and HR as soon as possible. Contoso Corp will consider rescission requests but is under no obligation to grant them, particularly where a replacement has already been identified, an offer has been extended to a successor, or the resignation has already been processed in payroll and benefits systems.

Notice periods run on calendar days from the date notice is received, not the date the employee next reports to work, and include any scheduled non-working days such as weekends within the two-week window. Approved PTO that falls within a notice period is generally honored as scheduled, consistent with the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy, unless business needs require the manager to ask the employee to adjust plans, in which case the manager and employee should discuss alternatives directly.

For employees on an approved leave of absence at the time they decide to resign, including parental, medical, or personal leave administered under the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy, the two-week notice guidance still applies where practical, but Contoso Corp recognizes that an employee on leave may not be able to physically return to hand off work, and the offboarding checklist should be adjusted accordingly by HR in coordination with the employee's manager.

Managers who receive a verbal or written resignation must notify their HR business partner within one business day. HR then sends the employee written confirmation of the accepted last day worked, an overview of next steps covering final pay, benefits continuation, retirement plan options, and equipment return, and a calendar invitation for an exit interview, so that the employee has a single, authoritative summary of the offboarding timeline from the outset.

Contoso Corp does not use garden leave as a standard practice, meaning that employees serving a notice period are generally expected to continue performing their normal duties rather than being paid to stay away from work, except where the manager and HR jointly determine that continued access poses a business or security risk, in which case the employee may be placed on paid administrative leave for the remainder of the notice period while retaining full pay and benefits through the agreed last day.

## Final Pay and Benefits Continuation

Final pay timing is governed by the wage payment laws of the state or country in which the employee is based, and Contoso Corp's payroll team applies the applicable rule automatically based on the employee's assigned work location. Employees based in Washington State, including the Northlake headquarters, receive their final paycheck no later than the next regularly scheduled payday following separation, whether the separation is voluntary or involuntary, consistent with Washington's wage payment statute.

Employees based in Texas, including the Austin office, likewise receive final pay by the next regular payday for a voluntary resignation. For an involuntary termination or a layoff, Texas law requires final pay within six calendar days of separation, and Contoso Corp's payroll team is instructed to process involuntary and layoff separations on an expedited off-cycle run so that this shorter deadline is met without exception.

Employees based in the Dublin office are paid out under Irish employment law and Contoso Corp's Republic of Ireland payroll provider, which requires that final wages, including any statutory or contractual entitlements, be settled through the normal payroll cycle in the pay period following termination, with a written statement of the final payment breakdown provided to the employee as required under Irish payslip regulations.

Final pay includes all earned and unpaid base wages or salary through the last day worked, any earned but unpaid commissions or bonuses that have vested according to the applicable incentive plan document, and reimbursement for any outstanding, properly submitted business expenses. Discretionary bonuses that have not yet been declared or paid as of the separation date are not owed unless the specific plan document states otherwise.

Accrued and unused paid time off is paid out in the final paycheck for all regular full-time and part-time employees, in accordance with the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy and the payout rules of the employee's work-location state, since several states, including Washington, treat accrued PTO as earned wages that must be cashed out at separation. Contoso Corp does not distinguish between voluntary and involuntary separation for purposes of PTO payout; any employee with a positive PTO balance at separation is paid for it.

Unused sick leave that was accrued under a jurisdiction's paid-sick-leave law, as opposed to Contoso Corp's discretionary PTO bucket, is handled according to that jurisdiction's specific rule; where local law does not require payout of statutory sick leave, Contoso Corp does not pay it out, and HR will identify which portion of an employee's balance falls into each category on the final pay statement provided at separation.

Group health, dental, and vision coverage under the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide ends on the last day of the month in which the employee's last day worked falls, unless the employee's plan or state requires an earlier end date. HR sends a COBRA election notice, or the equivalent continuation notice for Dublin-based employees under the relevant Irish or EU coordination-of-benefits rules, within the legally required window following the qualifying event, generally within fourteen days in the United States, so the employee can elect to continue coverage at their own cost.

Employees electing COBRA continuation coverage are responsible for the full premium, including the portion Contoso Corp previously subsidized, plus a permitted two percent administrative fee, and coverage can generally continue for up to eighteen months following separation, or longer in specific circumstances such as disability extensions, as detailed in the COBRA notice HR provides. Flexible spending account balances are generally forfeited at separation unless the employee elects COBRA continuation for the FSA itself, where permitted by the plan.

Life insurance, short-term disability, and long-term disability coverage provided under the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide terminate on the last day worked, though employees may have a right to convert group life insurance to an individual policy within thirty-one days of separation without evidence of insurability; HR includes conversion paperwork in the separation packet for employees who carry supplemental life coverage.

401(k) balances are not forfeited at separation. Employees may leave a vested balance in the Contoso Corp plan if it exceeds the plan's minimum balance threshold, roll it over to an individual retirement account or a new employer's plan, or, in limited circumstances, take a cash distribution subject to taxes and any applicable early-withdrawal penalty, all as described in the Contoso Corp Retirement Savings Plan Guide. Any unvested employer matching contributions are forfeited according to the plan's vesting schedule and returned to the plan's forfeiture account.

HR sends every departing employee a personalized retirement plan next-steps summary within five business days of the last day worked, generated in coordination with the plan's third-party recordkeeper, that shows the vested balance, available distribution options, and the applicable deadlines, and encourages the employee to consult the Contoso Corp Retirement Savings Plan Guide and a personal financial advisor before making a rollover or distribution decision.

Severance pay is offered to employees separated through a layoff or reduction in force, calculated under the current severance schedule of two weeks of base pay for each completed year of service, with a minimum of four weeks and a cap of twenty-six weeks, subject to the employee's signature on a separation and release agreement. Severance is not offered for resignations, retirements, or terminations for cause, and any exceptions require approval from the Chief People Officer.

Severance payments are issued as a lump sum through payroll within the first regular pay cycle following the effective date of the separation and release agreement, generally after the standard revocation period required under applicable age-discrimination law for employees signing a release has expired, and are subject to standard tax withholding rather than being treated as a tax-free benefit. Employees who do not sign the separation and release agreement within the offered consideration period do not receive severance but remain entitled to all other final pay and benefits described in this policy.

Employees separated through a layoff also receive a written notice describing the business reason for the reduction in force, their severance calculation, and, where a group layoff triggers notice obligations under the federal WARN Act or a comparable state law, the formal advance notice required by that statute. HR coordinates with Legal on every layoff exceeding the WARN Act threshold to confirm the notice period and content requirements are met before any affected employee is notified.

## Return of Company Property

All Contoso Corp property issued to an employee during their tenure must be returned no later than the employee's last day worked, or within five business days of that date for employees whose final work is remote and who need to ship equipment back rather than hand it over in person. This includes laptops, docking stations, monitors, mobile phones, security badges, access cards, parking passes, corporate credit cards, and any physical documents or files marked confidential.

For employees working from a Contoso Corp office in Northlake, Austin, or Dublin, the preferred process is an in-person handoff to the IT service desk or the employee's manager on the last day worked, at which point IT immediately logs the returned assets against the employee's equipment record in the asset management system and issues a signed confirmation receipt to both the employee and HR.

For fully remote employees or those working from a home office at the time of separation, HR's offboarding team emails a prepaid shipping label and packing instructions within one business day of the resignation or termination being processed. Equipment must be shipped within five business days of the last day worked, and Contoso Corp recommends the employee retain the shipment tracking number until IT confirms receipt.

Employees who fail to return company equipment within the applicable window receive a written reminder from HR, followed by an invoice for the equipment's depreciated replacement value if the equipment is not returned within thirty days of the last day worked, consistent with the property return acknowledgment every employee signs at hire. In cases of clear refusal to return property, Contoso Corp may pursue civil recovery, and Legal is looped in at that stage.

Physical security badges and access cards must be surrendered on the last day worked regardless of employee location; badge access is deactivated in the building access control system no later than end of business on that day, so an employee separating on short or no notice should expect building access to end immediately even if the equipment shipment for their laptop is still in transit.

Corporate credit cards are canceled by the Finance team on the last day worked, and any outstanding personal charges or unsubmitted business expenses must be reconciled by the employee within five business days; unresolved balances are handled according to the corporate card program's cardholder agreement and may be deducted from final pay to the extent permitted by the employee's work-location state law.

Company vehicles, if assigned, must be returned to the fleet coordinator with all keys, fobs, and toll transponders on or before the last day worked; mileage and condition are documented at handoff, and any damage beyond normal wear is assessed against the fleet policy rather than against the departing employee unless gross negligence is documented.

Software licenses, VPN tokens, and any hardware security keys issued for multi-factor authentication must be returned along with primary equipment. IT deactivates the employee's directory account, email mailbox, VPN access, and application single sign-on no later than the end of the last day worked, in accordance with the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy, and for involuntary terminations this deactivation is generally timed to occur during the separation meeting itself rather than at end of day.

Where an employee's role requires access to highly sensitive systems, such as production customer data, financial systems, or source code repositories, IT Security may deactivate that specific access immediately upon notice of resignation or termination, even before the employee's last day worked, while leaving standard email and calendar access intact so the employee can complete handoff communications; this graduated deactivation approach is documented in the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy.

Departing employees are reminded that any Contoso Corp data stored on personal devices, personal cloud storage, or personal email accounts must be deleted, and any confidential information must not be retained, forwarded, or used after separation, consistent with the confidentiality obligations in the employee's offer letter and the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy. IT may request written confirmation of deletion for employees who had elevated data access.

Personal belongings left in an office workspace, including items in a locked drawer or locker, are packed by the employee before departure wherever possible; for involuntary terminations where the employee leaves the premises the same day, HR or the facilities team packs remaining personal items and arranges pickup or shipment within ten business days at Contoso Corp's expense.

The offboarding checklist is not considered complete, and the employee's separation is not closed out in the HR information system, until IT confirms all equipment has been returned or invoiced, Finance confirms any corporate card or expense reconciliation is closed, and facilities confirms badge deactivation, ensuring that no departing employee retains active access to Contoso Corp systems or premises beyond the intended offboarding window.

## Exit Interview

Every employee separating from Contoso Corp, other than an employee who abandons their job without any contact, is invited to an exit interview conducted by Human Resources, never by the departing employee's direct manager, so that feedback can be shared candidly and without concern about affecting a working relationship or a future reference from that manager.

Exit interviews are scheduled to take place within the employee's final week, and are offered in whatever format the employee prefers among a live video call, an in-person meeting at a Contoso Corp office, or a written questionnaire, recognizing that some employees, particularly those separating involuntarily, may prefer not to have a live conversation.

The exit interview covers the employee's reasons for leaving, their experience with their manager and team, feedback on compensation and benefits, an assessment of career development and growth opportunities under the Contoso Corp Performance Management and Review Policy, and any concerns about policy compliance, workplace culture, or unresolved issues the employee wishes to raise.

Participation in the exit interview is voluntary, and an employee may decline to answer any specific question or may decline the exit interview altogether without any effect on final pay, benefits continuation, rehire eligibility, or reference checks; HR notes a decline in the offboarding record but does not follow up further unless the employee later reaches out.

HR aggregates exit interview themes on a quarterly basis and shares anonymized trends, such as recurring reasons for attrition within a department or recurring feedback about a specific manager, with the relevant department head and with the People team leadership; individual exit interview responses are not shared with the departing employee's manager in identifiable form except where the employee has explicitly consented or where the feedback concerns a legal or safety matter that HR is obligated to escalate.

For involuntary terminations and layoffs, the exit interview is reframed as an offboarding conversation focused primarily on confirming logistics, such as final pay, benefits continuation, and equipment return, rather than probing for qualitative feedback, though HR remains open to hearing concerns the employee wishes to share and documents them in the same manner as a voluntary exit interview.

Rehire eligibility is formally determined by HR, in consultation with the departing employee's manager, at the close of the offboarding process and is recorded in the HR information system as eligible, eligible with restrictions, or not eligible. Employees who resign in good standing with adequate notice are generally recorded as eligible; employees terminated for cause, or who abandoned their job, are generally recorded as not eligible, and a hiring manager considering a former employee for a new role must check this designation before extending an offer.

Contoso Corp maintains a neutral reference-check policy administered exclusively by HR. When a prospective employer or background-check vendor contacts Contoso Corp about a former employee, HR confirms only dates of employment, job title, and, if the former employee has provided written authorization, final base salary; HR does not comment on the reason for separation, performance history, or provide subjective opinions, and managers and coworkers are instructed to direct all reference inquiries to HR rather than responding personally.

An exception to the neutral-reference practice applies when a former employee has provided Contoso Corp with a signed release specifically authorizing a more detailed reference, or when HR is legally compelled to disclose additional information, such as in response to a subpoena; even in these cases, any substantive commentary is reviewed and approved by HR leadership before it is shared externally.

Employees returning company-provided access badges, IT equipment, and completing all steps described in this policy, including the exit interview or a documented decline of it, receive written confirmation from HR that their offboarding is complete, along with a copy of any COBRA election materials, retirement plan next-steps summary, and final pay statement, so that the employee has a single consolidated record of their separation from Contoso Corp.

This policy is reviewed annually by HR and Legal to confirm continued alignment with the wage payment, benefits continuation, and final-pay laws applicable in Washington, Texas, Ireland, and any other jurisdiction in which Contoso Corp maintains employees, and is updated as needed alongside related updates to the Contoso Corp Paid Time Off and Leave Policy, the Contoso Corp Health and Welfare Benefits Guide, the Contoso Corp Retirement Savings Plan Guide, and the Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy.
