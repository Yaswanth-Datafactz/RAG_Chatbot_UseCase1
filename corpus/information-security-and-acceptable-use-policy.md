# Contoso Corp Information Security and Acceptable Use Policy

## Purpose

Contoso Corp depends on the trust of its customers, partners, and employees to keep company information and systems secure. This policy sets out the minimum standards for how employees, contractors, interns, and vendors with system access must use Contoso Corp devices, networks, applications, and data, and it applies to every individual who is issued a contoso-corp.example account regardless of employment type, office location, or work arrangement.

This policy works alongside the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy, which governs how we collect, use, and protect personal data belonging to customers and employees, and the Contoso Corp Code of Conduct and Business Ethics Policy, which sets the broader expectations of honesty and professionalism that underpin all of our technology use. Where this policy and those policies overlap, the more restrictive requirement applies.

Information security at Contoso Corp is a shared responsibility. The Information Security team, led out of the Northlake headquarters with regional liaisons in Austin and Dublin, sets technical controls and monitors for threats, but no set of controls can substitute for employees making sound judgment about the systems and data they touch every day. Every employee is expected to read this policy in full during onboarding and to complete the annual security awareness training assigned through the learning management system.

This policy covers acceptable use of company-owned and personally owned devices used for work, authentication and password requirements, the handling of confidential and restricted data, and the process for reporting suspected security incidents. It does not attempt to enumerate every possible scenario; when a situation is not explicitly addressed, employees should apply the principle of least privilege and least exposure, and when in doubt, contact the Information Security team at security@contoso-corp.example before proceeding.

Managers are responsible for reinforcing these expectations within their teams and for promptly notifying Information Security and People Operations when a direct report's access should be modified or revoked, including during offboarding, role changes, or extended leave. Failure to do so can leave orphaned accounts and access rights active long after they are needed, which is one of the most common sources of preventable risk we see in internal audits.

Nothing in this policy is intended to restrict activity protected by applicable labor law, including discussions of wages, hours, or working conditions. Questions about the scope or interpretation of this policy should be directed to Information Security or to the Legal department, and employees who raise good-faith questions or concerns about security practices will not face retaliation, consistent with the non-retaliation commitments in the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook.

## Acceptable Use of Company Systems

Company systems, including laptops, mobile devices, email accounts, chat and collaboration tools, cloud storage, and internal applications, are provided to help employees perform their jobs. Incidental personal use is permitted, such as checking personal webmail during a break or sending a quick personal message, provided it is reasonable in frequency and duration, does not interfere with job performance, does not consume significant network or storage resources, and does not violate any other provision of this policy.

Company systems must never be used to operate a personal business, to solicit for outside organizations or causes without prior written approval from your manager and Corporate Communications, to gamble, to access, store, or transmit sexually explicit or otherwise unlawful content, or to engage in any activity that would violate local law in Washington, Texas, Ireland, or any other jurisdiction in which Contoso Corp operates. Use of company systems to harass, discriminate against, or intimidate coworkers is a violation of both this policy and the Contoso Corp Code of Conduct and Business Ethics Policy and will be handled under that policy's disciplinary framework.

Employees may install only software that has been approved through the IT Service Catalog or explicitly authorized by IT. Installing unlicensed, pirated, or cracked software on any company-owned device is strictly prohibited, both because it exposes Contoso Corp to legal liability for license infringement and because such software is a common vector for malware. Employees who need a tool that is not in the catalog should submit a request through the IT ticketing system rather than downloading it directly; most standard requests are reviewed within two business days.

Company email and chat platforms (currently Outlook and Microsoft Teams) are the default channels for business communication and are subject to monitoring and retention consistent with the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy and applicable legal hold obligations. Employees should not use personal email accounts or personal messaging apps such as WhatsApp or iMessage to conduct Contoso Corp business, because doing so moves company records outside of systems that Legal and Information Security can search, preserve, and secure.

Company-managed cloud storage, currently Microsoft OneDrive and SharePoint, is the approved location for storing work files, including drafts, spreadsheets, and presentations that contain internal or confidential information. Personal cloud storage services such as personal Google Drive, Dropbox, or iCloud accounts must never be used to store or synchronize Contoso Corp files, regardless of the sensitivity classification of the data involved, because Contoso Corp has no visibility into or control over the security posture of those personal accounts.

Bring-your-own-device use is permitted for email, calendar, and Teams chat on personal smartphones and tablets, provided the device is enrolled in the Contoso Corp mobile device management platform and meets minimum requirements: current operating system version, device passcode or biometric lock enabled, and remote wipe capability enabled. Personal computers may not be used to access confidential or restricted data, run development or production workloads, or connect directly to internal engineering systems; the Contoso Corp Remote Work and Flexible Work Arrangements Policy describes the equipment Contoso Corp provides so that BYOD use of laptops should rarely be necessary.

Removable media, including USB flash drives, external hard drives, and SD cards, may not be connected to company laptops or workstations except where Information Security has issued an encrypted device for a specific approved business purpose, such as transferring large design files between the Austin studio and an external print vendor. Unencrypted removable media presents a significant risk of both data loss and malware introduction, and USB ports on standard-issue laptops are configured to block unauthorized storage devices by default.

Employees must not attempt to disable, bypass, or tamper with security controls installed on company devices, including endpoint detection and response agents, disk encryption, mobile device management enrollment, or web content filtering, even when those controls are perceived as an inconvenience. Any employee who believes a control is materially interfering with their ability to do their job should raise the issue with IT rather than working around it; IT can often resolve the underlying friction without removing the protection.

Use of generative AI tools for work purposes is limited to those explicitly approved by Information Security and listed in the IT Service Catalog. Confidential or restricted data, including customer records, unreleased financial results, or source code, must never be entered into a public or unapproved AI tool, because doing so may transmit that data to a third party outside of any contractual protection Contoso Corp has negotiated. Employees with questions about whether a specific tool is approved should check the AI Tools page on the intranet or ask Information Security directly.

Network access on company premises in Northlake, Austin, and Dublin is segmented between a corporate network for company-managed devices and a guest network for visitors and personal devices; employees connecting a personal device to the office network should always use the guest network rather than requesting corporate credentials for it. Physical access to server rooms and network closets in all three offices is restricted to badge-authorized IT personnel, and any employee who observes an unfamiliar individual attempting to access these spaces should contact building security immediately.

Contoso Corp reserves the right to monitor, log, and review activity on company-owned systems and accounts to the extent permitted by applicable law, for purposes including security investigations, license compliance, and legal or regulatory obligations. This monitoring is conducted under the governance described in the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy and is not used to surveil ordinary, policy-compliant work activity.

## Password and Authentication Requirements

Every Contoso Corp account must be protected by a strong, unique password and, wherever the underlying system supports it, multi-factor authentication. Passwords must be a minimum of fourteen characters and may not be a dictionary word, a variation of the employee's name or username, or a password that has appeared in a known public breach; the identity platform checks new passwords against a breached-password list at the time they are set and will reject a match automatically.

Contoso Corp has moved away from mandatory periodic password rotation for accounts protected by multi-factor authentication, in line with current guidance from the National Institute of Standards and Technology, because forced rotation on a fixed schedule tends to produce weaker, more predictable passwords. Passwords must still be changed immediately if an employee suspects their credentials have been compromised, if Information Security notifies them of a suspected exposure, or if multi-factor authentication is not yet enabled on the account in question.

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all accounts that access company email, VPN, HR systems, financial systems, source code repositories, and any system classified as processing confidential or restricted data. The approved authenticator applications are Microsoft Authenticator and the Contoso Corp-issued hardware security key program available to engineering and finance staff on request; SMS-based one-time codes are supported only as a fallback for employees in regions with limited data connectivity and should not be selected as a primary method where an authenticator app is available.

Contoso Corp is migrating toward passkey-based, passwordless authentication for its core identity platform, and employees will be prompted to enroll a passkey on their company laptop or an approved authenticator app as this rollout reaches their team. Passkeys use public-key cryptography tied to the specific device on which they are created, which makes them resistant to phishing in a way that traditional passwords and even some one-time codes are not, and employees enrolled in the passkey program should keep at least one backup authentication method registered in case a device is lost or replaced.

Shared accounts and shared passwords are prohibited except for a small number of service accounts explicitly registered with Information Security and subject to additional monitoring; no employee should ever share their individual login credentials with a coworker, contractor, or manager, even temporarily to cover an absence. If a team needs shared access to a mailbox or system, the correct approach is to request shared or delegated access through IT rather than sharing a password.

Password managers are provided to all employees through the company-licensed enterprise password vault, and employees are strongly encouraged to use it to generate and store unique passwords for every account rather than reusing passwords across systems or writing them down. Storing work passwords in an unencrypted document, a spreadsheet, a sticky note, or a personal password manager that has not been approved by Information Security is a violation of this policy.

Session timeouts are enforced on company laptops (fifteen minutes of inactivity locks the screen) and on core business applications (idle sessions are terminated after sixty minutes). Employees must never disable automatic screen lock and must manually lock their screen, using the platform's lock shortcut, whenever they step away from their workstation, including in open office areas in Northlake, Austin, and Dublin where visual privacy from passersby cannot be guaranteed.

Privileged accounts, meaning accounts with administrative rights over servers, cloud infrastructure, network devices, or production databases, require both multi-factor authentication and a documented business justification on file with Information Security, and privileged access is reviewed quarterly to confirm it is still required. Engineers who need temporary elevated access for a specific task should request it through the just-in-time access request process rather than holding standing administrative rights.

Failed login attempts are monitored and accounts are automatically locked after ten consecutive failed attempts within a fifteen-minute window, requiring the employee to verify their identity through the IT help desk before the account is unlocked. Employees who are repeatedly locked out should not ask a coworker to share credentials as a workaround, and should instead contact the help desk directly, which can typically restore access within the same business day.

Authentication credentials for third-party tools that access Contoso Corp data, including SaaS vendors and integration partners, must be provisioned through the identity platform's single sign-on integration wherever the vendor supports it, rather than through a locally created username and password. This ensures that access to those tools is automatically revoked when an employee's Contoso Corp account is deactivated, closing a common gap that would otherwise leave former employees with lingering access to vendor systems.

Any employee who becomes aware of a security weakness in an authentication system, such as a password reset flow that appears to leak information about whether an email address has an account, should report it to security@contoso-corp.example rather than testing it further on production systems, consistent with the responsible disclosure expectations described in the reporting section of this policy.

## Handling Confidential Data

Contoso Corp classifies information into four tiers: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted. Public information is approved for release outside the company, such as published marketing materials and press releases. Internal information is intended for employees and authorized contractors but would cause limited harm if disclosed, such as internal org charts or meeting notes. Confidential information would cause meaningful competitive or reputational harm if disclosed, such as unreleased product roadmaps, customer contracts, and employee compensation data. Restricted information would cause severe harm, legal liability, or regulatory exposure if disclosed, such as authentication secrets, encryption keys, unreleased financial results prior to public announcement, and sensitive categories of personal data as defined in the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy.

Every document, dataset, and system at Contoso Corp should have an owner responsible for assigning its classification tier, and that classification should be indicated in the document's metadata, filename, or a visible header wherever practical. When the correct tier is unclear, employees should default to the more protective classification and confirm with the data owner or Information Security rather than guessing downward.

Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted both at rest and in transit. At rest, this means storing the data only in company-approved systems that provide encryption by default, such as the standard SharePoint and OneDrive environment, the corporate database platforms managed by IT, and encrypted laptop hard drives, all of which use full-disk encryption enabled automatically by device management at setup. In transit, this means transmitting the data only over TLS-encrypted connections, and never over unencrypted protocols such as plain FTP or unencrypted email attachments sent to external recipients.

Confidential and Restricted files that must be shared with an external party, such as a vendor, auditor, or law firm, should be shared through the company's approved secure file transfer or a time-limited, access-controlled sharing link, never as an unprotected email attachment. Before sharing Restricted data externally, the sender should confirm that a signed non-disclosure agreement or data processing agreement is on file with Legal, and should copy their manager for awareness on the first such exchange with a new external party.

Access to Confidential and Restricted systems and repositories is granted on a least-privilege, role-based basis and is reviewed at least twice per year by system owners in coordination with Information Security. Employees who change roles, teams, or projects should expect their access to be adjusted to match their new responsibilities, and should not retain access to systems from a prior role beyond a short transition period; managers are expected to flag such access for removal as part of the transfer process.

Printing of Confidential or Restricted material is discouraged and, where necessary, must be done using the release-based secure printing available in the Northlake, Austin, and Dublin offices, which holds a print job at the device until the employee authenticates with their badge, preventing sensitive documents from sitting unattended in an output tray. Printed Confidential or Restricted material that is no longer needed must be shredded using the locked shred bins located on each office floor rather than placed in ordinary recycling or trash.

Verbal discussion of Confidential or Restricted information, including customer names, financial figures, or personnel matters, should be limited to settings where it cannot be overheard by employees who do not have a business need to know, such as enclosed meeting rooms rather than open seating areas, and should never occur in public spaces such as building lobbies, elevators, cafes, or rideshares, even between two authorized employees.

Employees working from home or another remote location under the Contoso Corp Remote Work and Flexible Work Arrangements Policy must connect to the corporate VPN before accessing any internal system that hosts Confidential or Restricted data, and must ensure that screens displaying such data are not visible to household members, roommates, or anyone else sharing the workspace. Home network routers used for remote work should be secured with a unique administrative password and current firmware, and public Wi-Fi networks, such as those in coffee shops or airports, should never be used to access Confidential or Restricted systems without the VPN active.

Backups of systems containing Confidential or Restricted data are encrypted, access-controlled, and retained according to the schedule set out in the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy; employees do not need to create their own ad hoc backups of company data on personal devices or unapproved cloud services, and doing so creates an additional, unmanaged copy of sensitive data that falls outside company retention and deletion controls.

When Confidential or Restricted data is no longer needed for a legitimate business purpose, it should be deleted rather than retained indefinitely, consistent with the retention schedules maintained by the data owner and Legal. Company-owned devices and storage media that are retired, whether due to hardware refresh, employee departure, or equipment failure, must be returned to IT for certified data wiping or physical destruction rather than being discarded, resold, or repurposed by the individual employee.

Third-party vendors and contractors who require access to Confidential or Restricted Contoso Corp data must be under an active data processing or confidentiality agreement reviewed by Legal, and their access should be scoped as narrowly as possible and time-limited to the engagement. Business owners sponsoring a vendor relationship are responsible for notifying IT to deprovision vendor access promptly at the end of the engagement, in the same way they would for a departing employee.

## Reporting Security Incidents

A security incident is any suspected or confirmed event that could compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Contoso Corp information or systems, including a lost or stolen device, a suspected phishing email that was clicked or acted upon, unusual account activity, malware detected on a device, an unauthorized person in a secure area, or the accidental disclosure of Confidential or Restricted data to the wrong recipient. Employees are expected to report suspected incidents even when they are uncertain whether an event actually qualifies; Information Security would rather review and rule out a false alarm than learn about a real incident late.

Suspected incidents should be reported immediately to security@contoso-corp.example or through the twenty-four-hour security hotline, whose number is posted on the intranet home page and on placards near the entrance of every Contoso Corp office in Northlake, Austin, and Dublin. Employees who suspect an active, in-progress compromise, such as a ransomware message appearing on their screen, should also disconnect the affected device from the network, if it is safe and practical to do so, and should not attempt to remediate the issue themselves before Information Security is engaged.

Phishing emails can be reported directly from Outlook using the Report Phishing button embedded in the toolbar, which forwards the message to Information Security for analysis and automatically removes copies of the same message from other employees' mailboxes when a genuine campaign is confirmed. Employees who are unsure whether a message is legitimate should use this button rather than clicking any links or replying to the sender, and should never forward a suspected phishing email to coworkers as a warning, since that can itself spread a malicious link.

Contoso Corp runs a recurring phishing simulation program, coordinated by Information Security, that sends realistic but harmless simulated phishing messages to employees throughout the year. Employees who click a simulated phishing link are not disciplined for a first occurrence; instead they are automatically enrolled in a short refresher training module. Repeated failures across multiple simulations, however, are noted by the employee's manager and may result in additional required training or, in persistent cases, a broader performance conversation.

Once a report is received, Information Security triages the incident within one hour during business hours, or within four hours outside business hours for reports made through the hotline, and assigns it a severity level ranging from low to critical based on the scope of systems or data involved. Employees who report an incident can expect an acknowledgment and, where appropriate, follow-up questions from Information Security as part of the investigation, and should respond to those questions promptly and honestly even if the incident resulted from their own mistake.

For incidents classified as high or critical severity, Information Security follows a documented incident response process that includes containment, eradication, and recovery steps, and keeps affected business unit leaders informed throughout. Incidents that involve actual or suspected exposure of customer or employee personal data are escalated jointly to Legal and the Privacy Office under the notification procedures in the Contoso Corp Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy, which governs whether and how affected individuals and regulators must be notified.

Employees are never penalized for reporting a genuine mistake in good faith, such as accidentally emailing a spreadsheet to the wrong distribution list or losing a company laptop while traveling; prompt, honest reporting is treated as a mitigating factor, not an aggravating one, when Information Security and management evaluate the incident. Concealing a known incident, by contrast, or waiting to report it in the hope that it resolves itself, is treated as a serious violation of this policy in its own right, separate from whatever the underlying incident was.

Lost or stolen devices should be reported to security@contoso-corp.example and to the IT help desk as soon as an employee becomes aware, regardless of the time of day, so that the device can be remotely locked, located if possible, and wiped if it is not recovered within a short window. Employees traveling between the Northlake, Austin, and Dublin offices, or internationally, should report a lost device before their next flight or border crossing where possible, since device recovery and remote wipe become substantially harder once a device leaves the country of loss.

Physical security incidents, such as a propped-open secure door, a missing access badge, or an unfamiliar individual in a restricted area of any Contoso Corp office, should be reported to building security as well as to security@contoso-corp.example, since physical access to an office network jack or an unattended workstation can be just as damaging as a remote intrusion. Employees who lose their access badge should report it immediately so that it can be deactivated; a replacement badge is issued by the front desk in each office the same day in most cases.

Contoso Corp maintains a responsible disclosure channel for security researchers and employees who identify a vulnerability in a Contoso Corp product or system outside of the normal course of their job duties; reports can be sent to security@contoso-corp.example and will be acknowledged within two business days. Employees who discover a vulnerability incidentally, such as while testing an internal tool, should report it through this same channel rather than probing further or discussing it outside the need-to-know group investigating it.

Violations of this policy, whether resulting in an actual incident or not, are reviewed by Information Security in partnership with People Operations and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, consistent with the disciplinary standards described in the Contoso Corp Code of Conduct and Business Ethics Policy and the Contoso Corp Employee Handbook. Where a violation also implicates a legal or regulatory obligation, such as a data breach affecting customer personal data, Contoso Corp will additionally follow any required notification and remediation steps under applicable law.

This policy is reviewed at least annually by Information Security and may be updated to reflect new threats, new technology, or changes in applicable law; material changes are communicated to all employees by email and posted to the policy library on the intranet. Employees are responsible for reviewing updates when notified and for directing any questions about a change to security@contoso-corp.example.
